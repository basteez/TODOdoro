Direction 01
Midnight Canvas
Deep navy-grey canvas, warm amber devotion indicators, calm blue session accent. The night sky metaphor rendered as UI. Cards float with soft elevation; nothing competes with the canvas space. Dark-first, considered default. The experience feels like late-night focused work in a quiet room.

Direction 02
Obsidian
Near-pure black canvas with cool electric-blue session accents. More modern and minimal than Midnight Canvas — maximum contrast, no colour warmth, sharper card edges. Feels closer to a developer tool or a dark IDE. The coolness is intentional: focused, sharp, precise.
Dark primary Cool accent High contrast Sharper edges Modern / technical

Direction 03
Dusk
Deep indigo-purple canvas, warm orange devotion accents, muted purple session active state. The richest and most atmospheric of the dark directions. Feels like dusk or the last hour before night — transitional, introspective, warm but fading. More expressive than Midnight Canvas; slightly more emotionally saturated.

Direction 04
Fog Light
Warm off-white canvas, near-white elevated cards, amber devotion accents unchanged. The light mode companion to Midnight Canvas — same emotional character, inverted luminosity. Feels like a morning session, a lit desk, parchment and ink. Cards cast subtle shadows. Session accent is calm blue against the cream.
Light mode Warm cream Amber devotion Soft shadows Morning session feel

Direction 05
Monochrome
Near-black canvas, grey card surfaces, flat borderless card design. A single amber dot marks the most-invested todo — everything else is greyscale. Maximum restraint; the devotion amber is the only colour in the entire UI. Radical in its understatement. The amber dot becomes precious because it is the only thing allowed to have warmth.
Dark primary Greyscale Single amber accent Maximum restraint Radical minimalism

Direction 06
Depth
Deep navy canvas with cards that have strong physical elevation — layered shadows, inner highlights, gradient surfaces. Cards feel like objects you can pick up. The amber devotion dots glow faintly. More tactile than Midnight Canvas; heavier visual weight per card. Suits users who want the canvas to feel like a physical desk surface.
Dark primary Strong elevation Tactile / physical Glowing amber Higher visual weight
